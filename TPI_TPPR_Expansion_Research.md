# TPI / TPPR Multi-State Expansion Research for Building Code Consulting LLC (BCC)

**Prepared:** 2026-04-19
**Purpose:** Identify the highest-value US states/jurisdictions for expanding BCC's third-party inspection (TPI) and third-party plan review (TPPR) practice beyond DC / Northern VA / PG / Montgomery County.
**Method:** Statute review + official .gov registration page review. Marketing-only sources excluded. Items requiring direct verification before action are tagged **(verify)**.

---

## Executive Summary (Top-Line Findings)

The US TPI/TPPR market segments into three regulatory tiers:

1. **Statutory Private-Provider States** (full plan-review + inspection privatization permitted by state law) — **Florida** (most mature, ~24 years of practice) and now **New Jersey** (2023) and **Tennessee** (2024). These are the highest-leverage markets because both plan review and inspections can be sold as a complete service.
2. **Special-Inspection / Backstop States** (third-party allowed when AHJ is delayed, or for IBC Chapter 17 special inspections only) — **Texas** (HB 14, 2023, 15-day backstop), **Georgia** (HB 493, 2019, residential), **North Carolina** (NCGS Ch. 160D), **Massachusetts**, **Pennsylvania** (UCC TPA program is broader — opt-out municipalities), **South Carolina**, **NYC** (Special Inspection Agencies under 1 RCNY 101-06).
3. **Limited / Local-Only States** (no statewide framework; case-by-case) — most other states.

**Top-3 priority states for BCC** (by combined market size + ease of entry + revenue per project):
1. **FLORIDA** — best ROI, but requires FL-licensed PE/RA/BCA on staff and per-county registration in 8–10 counties.
2. **PENNSYLVANIA** — single statewide L&I certification covers all opt-in municipalities; broad scope (commercial + residential UCC).
3. **NORTH CAROLINA** — geographically adjacent, single-state PE comity is straightforward, Mecklenburg County alone has $20B+ annual permit volume.

Estimated minimum entry cost per priority state: **$2,000–$5,000** (PE comity + business registration + insurance rider + first jurisdiction fee). Estimated minimum timeline: **3–6 months** per state for full operational readiness.

## 2. State-by-State Detail

### 2.1 FLORIDA — Statutory Private Provider (DEEP DIVE)

**Statute:** [F.S. §553.791 — Alternative Plans Review and Inspection](https://www.leg.state.fl.us/Statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&URL=0500-0599/0553/Sections/0553.791.html)

**Who qualifies as a "Private Provider":** A FL-licensed Architect (Ch. 481), Engineer (Ch. 471), or Building Code Administrator (Ch. 468 Part XII) acting as agent of a registered Private Provider firm. Each firm must register **once with each AHJ** that has jurisdiction; there is no statewide "license" — instead, every county/city builds its own list using the same statutory minimums.

**Minimum statewide requirements (set by §553.791(18)):**
- Professional liability insurance: **$1M per claim / $1M aggregate** minimum (verify each AHJ — many require $2M)
- General liability + Workers Comp (per F.S. 440.02(8))
- Provider must not have financial interest in the project
- "Notice to Building Official Affidavit" + "Duly Authorized Representative Affidavit" filed at each permit

**Statewide registration:** None. Florida does NOT issue a statewide private-provider license. **You must register firm-by-firm with each AHJ.** This is the single biggest operational reality of the FL market.

### 2.2 TEXAS — HB 14 Backstop + Home-Rule Cities (DEEP DIVE)

**State-level statute:** [Texas Local Gov Code Ch. 245 + HB 14 (88R, 2023)](https://capitol.texas.gov/tlodocs/88R/analysis/html/HB00014E.htm) — Effective 9/1/2023.

**HB 14 mechanism:** If the AHJ does not approve/deny within 15 business days after the statutory deadline, the applicant can hire a third-party reviewer or inspector. The third-party must be **either ICC-certified for the trade, or a Texas-licensed PE**, or an existing AHJ inspector. The AHJ cannot charge an extra fee or require a waiver.

**TDLR registration (Industrialized Housing & Buildings only):** [tdlr.texas.gov/ihb/043ihb.htm](https://www.tdlr.texas.gov/ihb/043ihb.htm). Forms: IHB055 (Design Review Agency / TPIA), IHB105 (TPI). This is **only for modular/manufactured buildings** — not site-built.

### 2.4 PENNSYLVANIA — Statewide TPA (One-and-Done)

**Statute:** [34 Pa. Code Ch. 401–405 (UCC)](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dli/programs-services/labor-management-relations/bureau-of-occupational-and-industrial-safety/tpa-buildings).

**Best feature:** A single PA Department of Labor & Industry "Third Party Agency" certification covers ALL opt-in municipalities (most of PA). No per-county registration needed.

**Application requirements** ([Certification of Individuals and TPAs](https://www.dli.pa.gov/ucc/Pages/Certification-of-Individuals-and-Third-Party-Agencies.aspx)):
- Completed application + fee (verify current fee schedule)
- Proof of E&O / professional liability insurance: **$1M minimum**
- Designation of certified individual code officials in each discipline (Building, Mech, Elec, Plumb, Fire, Energy, Accessibility) — each individual must hold a separate PA UCC certification

**Contact:** UCCBOIS@pa.gov / 717-787-6085. Office: 651 Boas St, Room 1606, Harrisburg PA 17121.

**Bottom line:** PA is the highest-leverage entry per dollar — one application = statewide reach. Major catch: **each "code official" employee** must individually hold PA UCC certifications (which generally require ICC certs + PA exam). For BCC, this means hiring or partnering with at least one PA-certified code official per trade.

### 2.6 NEW JERSEY — DCA Authorized Private On-Site Inspection Agencies

**Statute:** [Assembly Bill 573 (2023)](https://www.gibbonslawalert.com/2023/02/08/new-jersey-adopts-private-construction-inspection-bill/) amending the Uniform Construction Code Act.

**Mechanism:** When the local construction official cannot inspect within **3 business days**, the owner may use an Authorized Private Inspection Agency from the [DCA's published list](https://www.nj.gov/dca/codes/publications/pdf_ora/onsite_agency_list.pdf).

**Application:** Through the [DCA Service Portal](https://njdcaportal.dynamics365portals.us/) → Codes & Standards → Private Onsite Inspection Agency Application. Must designate licensed sub-code officials (NJ Building / Electrical / Plumbing / Fire / Elevator) — each is a separate NJ-issued credential.

**Insurance:** $1M professional liability minimum (verify current rule).
**Renewal:** Annual.

**Bottom line:** NJ is closer to PA's model (single statewide list) but requires NJ-issued sub-code official credentials per discipline — those require NJ-specific exams beyond ICC. Higher entry barrier than PA.

### 2.7 TENNESSEE — SFMO Third-Party Registration (NEW 2024)

**Statute:** [Public Chapter 771 (2024)](https://lgwmlaw.com/tennessees-new-third-party-review-and-inspection-law-impact-on-design-and-construction-professionals/) → [Rule 0780-02-09 (revised July 2025)](https://publications.tnsosfiles.com/rules/0780/0780-02/0780-02-09.20250714.pdf).

**Mechanism:** TN-licensed PE/Architect (or trade-certified pro) registers with the State Fire Marshal's Office. Local AHJ has 10 business days to approve/deny third-party submittal; if no response, escalates to SFMO with another 10-day clock.

**Scope:** Only applies in **state-exempt jurisdictions** (i.e., where SFMO is the AHJ; about 60% of TN territory) — but that's a huge market. Commercial + residential building codes + electrical.

**Registration page:** [tn.gov 3rd-Party FAQ](https://www.tn.gov/commerce/fire/3rd-party/faq.html). New program — fee structure being finalized (verify).

**Bottom line:** Brand-new market with low competition. Worth tracking for 2026–2027 if BCC wants early-mover advantage. TN PE comity ~$100 + NCEES Record.

### 2.8 SOUTH CAROLINA — Statewide Special Inspector Registration

**Statute:** [SC Code Title 6 Ch. 9](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/code/t06c008.php) + [SC Building Codes Council CRR 8-700](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/coderegs/Chapter%208.pdf).

**Application:** [SCLLR Special Inspectors Page](https://llr.sc.gov/bcc/siinfo.aspx). $50 application fee. Required: copies of ICC (or other recognized cert org) certifications. **Architects and PEs licensed in SC are exempt from registration** statewide — but per BCC FAQ, individual local jurisdictions may still require the registration; file the $50 anyway as belt-and-suspenders.

**Renewal:** **Biennial** (corrected 2026-04-27 per LLR site verification — earlier note said annual, that was wrong). Cycle expires June 30 of odd-numbered years; current cycle 2025-2027.

**Bottom line:** Cheapest entry in the country at $50. If BCC has SC PE, no registration even needed. Charleston specifically [accepts third-party inspections](https://www.charleston-sc.gov/1518/Third-Party-Building-Inspections).

### 2.9 NEW YORK CITY — DOB Special Inspection Agency

**Rule:** [1 RCNY §101-06](https://www.nyc.gov/site/buildings/codes/special-inspections.page); registration via [DOB NOW: Licensing](https://www.nyc.gov/site/buildings/industry/sia-registration.page).

**Three classes:**
- **Class 1** — Maximum scope (structural steel, concrete, soils, masonry, etc.) — requires AISC/ICC accreditation + PE/RA on staff + accredited testing lab affiliation.
- **Class 2** — Intermediate scope (non-structural special inspections).
- **Class 3** — Limited scope (energy code, mechanical, etc.).

**Insurance:** Per 1 RCNY 101-06(c)(7) — typically $1M GL + $1M professional + WC (verify exact figures).

**TR1 form:** Filed per project to take responsibility for specific inspection categories.

**Bottom line:** Highest revenue per project (NYC commercial structural SI work bills $5–25k+ per project), but highest barrier. Class 1 typically requires partnership with an accredited testing lab. **For BCC's first year, target Class 3 (energy, mechanical) which has lower barriers.**

### 2.10 MASSACHUSETTS — TPIA under 780 CMR

**Code:** [780 CMR 110.R3 / 110.R4](https://www.mass.gov/massachusetts-state-building-code-780-cmr).

Two main MA opportunities:
1. **TPIA registration** for manufactured/modular building inspection (state-issued, annual).
2. **Special Inspections under IBC Ch. 17** as adopted by 780 CMR — performed by registered design professionals (MA PE or RA).

**Bottom line:** Smaller TPI market than NY/NJ; primarily structural SI work for commercial. MA PE comity is more demanding (requires NCEES Record; ~$200 board fee; 6–10 weeks).

## 4. Action Plan — Recommended Order of Operations

### 4.1 Recommended FIRST state: **PENNSYLVANIA**

**Why:**
- Single statewide certification = highest geographic ROI per dollar spent.
- BCC's existing MD/DC presence makes PA a natural geographic next-door extension (Baltimore-Harrisburg corridor).
- PE comity is fast (PA accepts NCEES Record; ~$50 board fee; ~4 weeks).
- PA UCC scope includes both plan review AND inspection AND code consulting — same business model BCC already runs in DC.
- Major PA opt-in metros: Philadelphia (mostly opted out, but suburbs strong), Pittsburgh metro, Lehigh Valley, Harrisburg, Lancaster.

**90-day PA action checklist:**
1. **Week 1–2:** Establish NCEES Record (no fee). Request transcript verification from your PE-issuing university.
2. **Week 2–4:** Submit PE comity application to PA SPELS Board ($50 + transmittal).
3. **Week 4–6:** Register Building Code Consulting LLC as foreign LLC with PA Dept of State (~$250).
4. **Week 6–8:** Identify a PA-UCC-certified code official to partner with (or take PA exams yourself for at least Building + Plan Review categories — $50 each).
5. **Week 8–10:** Submit PA L&I TPA application (UCCBOIS@pa.gov) with $1M E&O COI + fee.
6. **Week 10–12:** Once issued, market to opt-in PA municipalities directly (start with Lancaster, York, Cumberland, Lehigh — high construction volume, no major existing TPA competition).

### 4.2 Recommended SECOND state: **FLORIDA**

**Why:** Largest market, highest fee per project, mature private-provider acceptance. Also where BCC's MEP+B+FP combo is most valuable (Florida is hurricane-driven inspection-heavy market).

**Florida action checklist (run in parallel with PA after PA application is submitted):**
1. NCEES Record → FL Board of Professional Engineers comity (~$250, 6–10 weeks).
2. Optional but recommended: FL Building Code Administrator (BCA) license — Ch. 468 Part XII — apply via DBPR.
3. FL Sunbiz foreign LLC registration (~$125).
4. FL DBPR business license (verify category — typically not needed if PE/RA-led firm).
5. Register firm with **5 priority counties** in this order: **Lee → Orange → Hillsborough → Miami-Dade → Pinellas** (cheapest/easiest first; Miami-Dade has the most paperwork).
6. Build $2M E&O policy (FL §553.791(18) minimum is $1M but most major counties prefer $2M).

### 4.3 Recommended THIRD state: **NORTH CAROLINA** (Mecklenburg-only first)

Single-county pilot:
1. NC PE comity (~$100 + NCEES, 4–8 weeks).
2. NC SOS foreign LLC (~$250).
3. Mecklenburg SI Program registration ($99). Apply at [meck-si.com](https://meck-si.com/msi/default.aspx).

## 5. Risks & Things to Verify Before Acting

1. **(verify)** Each FL county fee schedule — many show "no fee" for one-time firm registration but charge per-permit "private provider fees." Read each county's PP fee table before quoting jobs.
2. **(verify)** TX HB 14 implementation — many AHJs are still building their internal third-party processes. Some are slow-walking it. Call each city's Dev Services to confirm they have a formal acceptance procedure.
3. **(verify)** PA UCC TPA fee schedule — current as of 2024; rate may have updated.
4. **(verify)** TN SFMO program is brand new; rule was revised July 2025; fee structure and registration portal may not be fully online.
5. **(verify)** NC NCGS 160D allows owner-elected TPPR but many AHJs still resist. Mecklenburg is the safest first market.
6. **(verify)** NJ DCA list as of February 2026 — A.B. 573 implementation is still maturing; confirm latest list and acceptance rate.
7. **PE Liability:** Adding 5 states expands your PE liability footprint. Confirm with E&O broker that policy covers all states where you hold a PE — most do but some policies have territory exclusions.
