# Fairfax County Expedited Building Plan Review Program (EBPR) — Briefing for Yue Cao, PE, MCP

Prepared 2026-04-27 for the Fairfax County training/test sessions on 2026-04-28 (MEP, 8 a.m.–12 p.m. MS Teams) and 2026-04-29 (Fire Protection, 8–10 a.m. MS Teams). KCY Engineer PLLC is the sponsoring firm.

## 1. Program Name + Official Process

- **Official name:** Expedited Building Plan Review Program (EBPR). Reviewers are titled **Certified Peer Reviewers** (CPR), sometimes shown as "Designated Peer Reviewer."
- Program page: https://www.fairfaxcounty.gov/landdevelopment/expedited-building-plan-review-program
- Public list: https://www.fairfaxcounty.gov/landdevelopment/expedited-building-plan-review-program/certified-peer-reviewer-list
- Goal: cut commercial plan-review time roughly in half. Available for all project types, "most effective for commercial tenant fit-outs."
- Operated by Fairfax County Land Development Services (LDS), Building Plan Review Division. Note: this is **distinct** from the *site plan* expedited review run with ESI / DPE (Chapter 117) — that is a different program for civil/site plans.

## 2. Reviewer Qualifications

- **License:** Valid, current Virginia license as Professional Engineer **or** Architect (DPOR).
- **Experience:** Minimum 5 years designing, checking, and reviewing using the code(s) for the discipline sought, plus 3 professional references.
- **ICC certifications by discipline (Commercial):**
  - Building — Building Plans Examiner + Accessibility Inspector/Plans Examiner
  - Mechanical — Mechanical Plans Examiner + Commercial Energy Plans Examiner
  - Plumbing — Plumbing Plans Examiner + Accessibility Inspector/Plans Examiner
  - Electrical — Electrical Plans Examiner + Commercial Energy Plans Examiner
  - Fire — Fire Plans Examiner + Building Plans Examiner
  - Structural — Building Plans Examiner (Structural Complex covers any height, retention/shoring; Structural is up to 4 stories)
  - Residential — Residential Building Inspector + Residential Energy Inspector/Plans Examiner
- **Mandatory Fairfax initial instruction session** (by appointment with EBPR Manager, 703-324-1087).
- **Ongoing:** at least **2 round-table sessions per calendar year** (Fri 8–9:15 a.m., Herrity Bldg Room 106) and **minimum 4 plans peer-reviewed per fiscal year (Jul 1–Jun 30)** to maintain status.
- Insurance is **not** explicitly listed as a requirement on the program page (not findable in the public materials reviewed).

## 3. Disciplines Accepted

Building, Structural (and Structural Complex), Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing, Fire, Residential, plus Commercial/Residential Energy embedded within MEP. Yue's relevant tracks for this week: **Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing (Day 1)** and **Fire (Day 2)**. Energy compliance is bundled into Mechanical and Electrical via the Commercial Energy Plans Examiner cert.

## 4. Reviewer Role

The CPR is hired and paid by the owner/applicant, works **independently of the design team**, and:

1. Reviews the construction documents against the applicable codes.
2. Iterates with the design team until compliant; functions as a private reviewer, **does not** perform design.
3. **Certifies** (recommends) the drawings to the County once compliant — i.e., signs/stamps a peer-review certification that the documents meet code.
4. The County still performs its own review but gives peer-reviewed packages **highest priority**; County reviewers may still find additional deficiencies or missing items.
5. Must disclose any conflict of interest to the Building Official before accepting a job.

The CPR does **not** replace the County stamp; County issues the permit. Simple corrections to County comments do not need re-peer-review; **complex changes do**, and post-permit revisions must be peer-reviewed.

## 5. Process Flow

Applicant-led: Owner/GC selects a CPR from the public list → contracts and pays the CPR directly → CPR reviews and certifies → applicant submits to Fairfax LDS via PLUS portal flagged as peer-reviewed → County prioritizes review → permit issued. Fairfax does not publish a fixed turnaround SLA; messaging is "review times cut in half."

## 6. Code References (effective in VA since 2024-01-18)

- Virginia Construction Code (VCC) 2021 — base IBC 2021 with VA amendments
- IMC 2021, IPC 2021, IFGC 2021, IECC 2021, IFC 2021, IRC 2021
- NEC = NFPA 70-2020
- Sprinkler/standpipe/fire pump/fire alarm: NFPA 13, 14, 20, 72 (editions per VCC reference)
- Accessibility: ICC/ANSI A117.1
- VCC overrides: Part I of 13VAC5-63 (Virginia Administrative Code); see VA-specific amendments to IBC Ch. 1 (admin), egress provisions, and definitions of Building Official authority. Verify current edition references each project at https://law.lis.virginia.gov/admincodefull/title13/agency5/chapter63/

## 8. Common Pitfalls / Grounds for Removal

Per published program rules, Fairfax may revoke certification when a CPR:

- Recommends drawings that do not meet code (recurring missed items in County re-review).
- Fails to follow program guidelines or maintain ICC + VA license.
- Falls below 4 peer-reviewed plans per fiscal year.
- Reviews plans designed by their own firm, or holds undisclosed financial interest with the owner/developer/contractor (conflict of interest).
- Submits "complex changes without peer review."
- Skips required round-tables.

Reinstatement is allowed only **9+ months** after removal.
