## 1. Statutory & Code Basis

- **NCGS Ch. 160D Article 11** — owner-elected TPI/TPPR.
- **NCBC Chapter 17** — Special Inspections framework.
- Buncombe forms reference NC State Building Code throughout.

## 2. Program Overview

Buncombe County publishes three named SI documents on its Forms & Reports page:
1. **Special Inspection and Testing Agreement** (with embedded Schedule of Special Inspections)
2. **Registered Design Professional Inspection Form** — explicitly the only form Buncombe accepts for RDP component/element inspections; once submitted, the County is "discharged and released from liabilities" related to that component.
3. **Element Inspection Procedure** — guidance doc

Also published:
- **Special Inspections Acknowledgement** (older PDF on legacy buncombecounty.org)
- **Inspection Procedures for Residential and Commercial**
- **Registered Design Professional Inspection Form** (legacy mirror on buncombecounty.org)

## 4. Eligibility

- **NC-registered PE or Architect** seals the RDP Inspection Form. "No other form will be accepted for registered design professional inspections" — Buncombe is strict.
- Special Inspection & Testing Agreement signed by owner, GC, NC PE/RA (third-party), and AHJ.
- ICC certs for non-RDP field inspectors handling Ch. 17 SI work.

## 8. Plan Review / Statement / Final Report Format

- **Schedule of Special Inspections** is embedded inside the Special Inspection and Testing Agreement.
- **Final Report / Sign-off** = the **Registered Design Professional Inspection Form** signed and sealed by the NC PE/RA. This is the only form Buncombe accepts for RDP component inspections — explicit policy.
- Once filed, "such inspections will be noted in all inspection records including the Certificate of Occupancy or Certificate of Compliance."

## 9. Submission Process

1. KCY's NC PE engages with owner/GC; both execute the Special Inspection and Testing Agreement.
2. Agreement + embedded Schedule submitted to Buncombe Permits & Inspections at permit issuance.
3. Field SI work performed during construction.
4. **For each component/element:** RDP files the Registered Design Professional Inspection Form (sealed) — releases County from liability for that element.
5. CO issued; SI records attached to permit file.
