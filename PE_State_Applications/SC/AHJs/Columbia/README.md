# Columbia, SC — Third-Party Inspection / Special Inspector

**AHJ:** City of Columbia Planning & Development — Building Inspections Division
**Verified:** 2026-04-27

---

## 1. Overview

The City of Columbia operates standard IBC Chapter 17 special inspections (per SC adoption) but does **NOT publish a formal "Third-Party Inspection Program" page or pre-qualification process at the city level**. Special inspections in Columbia follow the SC LLR statewide framework — the owner directly engages SC LLR-registered Special Inspectors (or SC-licensed PE/RA acting under §6-8-40).

**Per Columbia Planning & Development website (verified 2026-04-27):**
> "Special inspections may be required by the Building Code (IBC Chapter 17) for certain projects... Where required by this chapter, the registered design professional in responsible charge shall prepare a statement of special inspections to be submitted with permit application. The owner, or owner's authorized agent, other than the contractor, shall employ one or more approved agencies to provide special inspections and tests during construction."

**Bottom line for KCY:** No separate Columbia pre-qualification process. Once KCY holds SC PE comity + SC LLR SI registration, KCY can pitch directly to Columbia owners/architects for IBC Ch. 17 special inspections. Columbia building inspectors retain Ch. 1 inspections; KCY would handle Ch. 17 SI scope only.

**Status:** NEEDS_OUTREACH — confirm with Building Official whether ANY pre-qualification or registration is required at the city level beyond SC LLR statewide registration.

## 3. Eligibility

Per IBC Ch. 17 as adopted in SC + SC §6-8-40:
- SC LLR-registered Special Inspector, OR
- SC-licensed PE or RA in the discipline (§6-8-40 statutory exemption)

City of Columbia does not appear to layer additional credentialing on top.

---

## 4. Insurance

Not specified at city level — IBC default applies. Owner's contract with SI agency typically dictates COI minimums; expect $1M GL / $1M E&O baseline.

---

## 5. Fees

**No city registration fee.** Only SC LLR statewide $50 SI registration fee (covered in `SC_LLR_Statewide/README.md`).

---

## 6. Required Submission Docs

For each project (not for inspector pre-qualification):
- Statement of Special Inspections — prepared by registered design professional, submitted with permit application (per Columbia's published text above).
- Per-inspection reports filed with Building Inspections Division.

### Forms downloaded to `forms/`

None — no city-level pre-qualification form exists. Use SC LLR `Special_Inspection_Form.pdf` for the project-level Statement of Special Inspections.

---

## 7. Plan Review Report / SI Report Formats

City of Columbia does not publish a mandated SI report template. Use the SC LLR statewide form or the inspector agency's standard format (with project ID + permit number).

---

## 8. Submission Process

1. Owner engages SI agency (KCY) directly — no city pre-qualification.
2. RDP files Statement of Special Inspections with permit app.
3. KCY performs inspections, files reports per project.
4. Schedule city-staff inspections via Access Portal (cut-off 3:00 PM previous day; same-day not available).
