# Charleston, SC — Third-Party Building Inspection Program (TPI)

**AHJ:** City of Charleston Building Inspections Division (BID)
**Verified:** 2026-04-27

---

## 1. Overview

Charleston is the **only major SC city with a published, formal Third-Party Inspection (TPI) program** at the city level. Program established September 2021. Statutory backstop: SC Code Title 6 Ch. 9 + IBC Ch. 17 as adopted in SC.

**Per the city's TPI page (verified 2026-04-27):**
> "The purpose of the City of Charleston Building Inspections Division (BID) Third Party Inspection Program (TPI) is to establish [criteria for] when third party inspections can be utilized, the minimum criteria to become a pre-qualified third party inspector, the minimum criteria to maintain pre-qualification as a third party inspector, and the processes of conducting TPI."

**Critical caveat:** "Third-party inspections are not allowed by right and shall be approved by the Chief Building Official prior to commencement of construction."

**Bottom line for KCY:** Charleston is the **target SC market**. Pre-qualification + per-project Chief Building Official approval = two-step process. PE/RA exemption applies but pre-qualification submittal still required.

## 3. Eligibility

Per the TPI page (full criteria in the program policy PDF):
- "minimum criteria to become a pre-qualified third party inspector"
- "minimum criteria to maintain pre-qualification"

The published page does not enumerate specifics. Based on standard SC AHJ practice (mirroring Greenville County), pre-qualification almost certainly requires:

- SC PE OR SC RA OR SC LLR Special Inspector registration (per SC Code §6-8-40)
- ICC certifications matching the inspection categories requested
- Government photo ID
- Per-project chief-building-official approval (separate from pre-qualification)

## 7. Plan Review Report / SI Report Formats

Charleston has its **own Acknowledgement Form** referenced on the TPI page; format of the per-inspection report is defined inside the 2021 program policy PDF. Charleston does NOT separately offer a TPPR (third-party plan review) program at the city level — plan review is handled by city staff.

---

## 8. Submission Process

1. Pre-qualification: Submit pre-qualification packet to Building Inspections Division (BID).
2. Per-project approval: Submit TPI request + Acknowledgement Form to Chief Building Official **prior to commencement of construction**.
3. Conduct inspections, file reports per policy format.
4. Final inspection still requires city-staff sign-off (TPI augments, does not replace, IBC Ch. 1 city inspections per the page text).
