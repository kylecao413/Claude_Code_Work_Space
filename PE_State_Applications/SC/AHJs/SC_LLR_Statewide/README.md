# SC LLR Statewide — Special Inspector Registration

**AHJ:** SC Department of Labor, Licensing and Regulation — Building Codes Council (BCC)
**Verified:** 2026-04-27

---

## 1. Overview

South Carolina runs a **statewide Special Inspector Registration** through the SC LLR Building Codes Council (CRR Chapter 8, Article 7). This is the single most important credential for SC TPI work — it is recognized by every SC AHJ that accepts third-party inspections (Charleston, Greenville County, Greenville City, etc.).

**Statutory basis:** SC Code Ann. § 6-9 (Building Codes); CRR 8-700 (Special Inspectors). PE/RA exemption codified at **SC Code Ann. § 6-8-40**.

**Per BCC FAQ (verified 2026-04-27):**
> "Professional Engineers and Architects registered in South Carolina can perform special inspections without obtaining a Special Inspector registration, per S.C. Code Ann. §6-8-40. However, local jurisdictions may impose additional requirements and require the registration anyway. Compliance with local jurisdiction requirements is necessary."

**Bottom line for KCY:** Once Yue Cao's SC PE comity issues, KCY is statutorily exempt from the $50 SI registration. **However**, several local AHJs (e.g., Greenville County) still require the SI registration on top of the PE — so we should **still file the $50 SI app** as belt-and-suspenders.

## 3. Eligibility

Two pathways (per LLR exams.aspx):

1. **Certification Route** — pass required exams and/or hold required ICC certs per category in the Special Inspector Manual.
2. **Engineer-in-Training Route** — EIT (passed FE) + 1 year direct supervision by SC PE in the relevant category (signed affidavit).

**Statutory exemption (PE/RA):** SC PE or SC RA can act as Special Inspector in their discipline without registering — but local AHJs may still require the registration, so we register anyway.

### Special Inspector Categories (per Special Inspector Manual, 2009)
Categories include: Soils, Reinforced Concrete, Structural Steel & Bolting, Welding, Masonry, Spray-Applied Fireproofing, Smoke Control, EIFS, Wood Construction, Pier Construction, Pile Driving, etc. — full list in the Manual PDF (download below).

For **KCY's first SC project mix** (commercial code-compliance inspection + plan review), the most relevant category is the IBC Ch. 17 line items KCY/BCC would actually perform — which Yue's PE Fire Protection + MEP background covers via the PE-exemption pathway.

## 4. Insurance

**No state-mandated minimum E&O for SC SI registration** — LLR does not require an insurance certificate to register. Local AHJs may impose insurance minimums per project (Charleston program does — see Charleston README).

## 7. Plan Review Report / SI Report Formats

SC LLR does **not** publish a mandated SI report template at the state level — local AHJ format applies (Charleston has its own; Greenville County has its own). The state-level "Statement of Special Inspections" PDF (above) is a project-application form (filled by the Registered Design Professional, attached to permit), **not** a per-inspection report template.
