## 1. Overview

Frisco's Building Inspections Division does **NOT publish a formal third-party plan review or inspection registration program** under HB 14 (88R, 2023). Instead, Frisco's published "Third Party" mentions relate to **SB 1202 (home backup power)** — i.e., 3rd-party raters for residential battery backup installations — which is unrelated to BCC's commercial TPR/TPI scope.

Frisco does require **Contractor Registration** through its eTRAKiT portal for any firm performing work in the city. The "3rd party rater" trade (SB 1202 narrow scope) is one of the registered trades. General TPR/TPI under HB 14 has no published registration form — practical TPR/TPI work happens via the HB 14 statewide backstop (15-business-day rule) on a per-project basis.

This is the **softest of the 7 TX AHJs** alongside Austin. Most opportunity here is HB 14 backstop work.

## 3. Eligibility

For Contractor Registration in eTRAKiT:
- Company info + contact info
- Insurance: General Liability with City of Frisco (6101 Frisco Square Blvd, Frisco TX 75034) as certificate holder; **Engineer of Record requires $1M per occurrence minimum**
- ACORD 25 form
- Trade selection (3rd party rater is one option — narrow SB 1202 scope)

For HB 14 backstop (general TPR/TPI):
- TX-licensed PE OR ICC cert per trade
- No formal Frisco registration today
